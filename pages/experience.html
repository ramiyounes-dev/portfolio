Publications

Impact of verbal instructions and deictic gestures of a cobot on the performance of human coworkers

Nov 2024 | Nancy, France

HUMANOIDS

This paper investigates the effectiveness and eff iciency of incorporating pointing gestures as well as handspeech synchronization policies into instruction delivery, as would be used in an industrial case with a cobot. Through brick assembly tasks, our study explores the integration of pointing gestures into human-robot interaction, extending prior research on verbal instruction efficacy. Results show that pointing gestures significantly reduce errors compared to verbal instructions alone, especially for complex tasks. However, this improvement comes at the cost of increased task completion time. We also show that depending on this synchronization, the user might delay its action until all information is presented instead of exploiting the information as it arrived. This study emphasizes the potential of pointing gestures and hand-speech synchronization in improving human-robot interaction and suggests further research for optimal integration.

Multimodal Human-Robot Collaboration in shared tasks

Sep 2024 | Grenoble, France

École doctorale électronique, électrotechnique, automatique, traitement du signal

This work investigates the dynamics of human-robot interaction by examining how both verbal and co-verbal communication affect collaborative task execution. We equip the robot with hierarchical planning abilities. This enables the robot to break down tasks into manageable subtasks, naming them as it progresses through its planned actions.In our investigation, we focus on a specific scenario where the human participant is engaged in object manipulations, similar to those encountered in assembly tasks, with an industrial robot orchestrating the plan. Central to our approach is the structuring and parameterization of the verbalization space. This involves careful selection of semantic, syntactic, and lexical elements to articulate the elementary manipulations and introduce subtasks. To achieve this, we devised a new crowdsourcing methodology tailored to our research needs.Subsequently, we conduct in-person experiments to empirically evaluate the impact of both verbal and co-verbal communication, particularly through deictic arm gestures, on task performance. Our findings underscore the significance of communication modalities in enhancing collaborative task execution within human-robot interactions.

Automatic Verbal Depiction of a Brick Assembly for a Robot Instructing Humans

Jul 2022 | Edinburgh, UK

SIGDIAL

Verbal and nonverbal communication skills are essential for human-robot interaction, in particular when the agents are involved in a shared task. We address the specific situation when the robot is the only agent knowing about the plan and the goal of the task and has to instruct the human partner. The case study is a brick assembly. We here describe a multi-layered verbal depictor whose semantic, syntactic and lexical settings have been collected and evaluated via crowdsourcing. One crowdsourced experiment involves a robot instructed pick-and-place task. We show that implicitly referring to achieved subgoals (stairs, pillows, etc) increases performance of human partners.
